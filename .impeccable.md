# .impeccable.md

Design context for Fabricio Pirini's portfolio. This file is the source of truth for all design decisions.

---

## Design Context

### Users

High-caliber companies and individuals hiring experienced senior engineers — not volume recruiters scanning resumes, but people who care enough to click through. They arrive skeptical and time-poor. The job is to make them feel something genuine within 10 seconds: curiosity first, then confidence. Secondary audience: freelance clients looking for a design-aware engineer who won't just "follow a Figma" but will push back with taste.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: Authentic, crafted, playful.

- **Authentic**: No corporate voice, no AI-generated blandness. Fabricio sounds like a real person with opinions and quirks. The LEGO metaphor, the Fab coins, the direct copy — all of it is intentional personality, not decoration.
- **Crafted**: This is a portfolio from someone who cares about Design Engineering — the seam between code and design. Every animation, every spacing decision, every hover state should look intentional. Craft shows in the details others skip.
- **Playful**: Not silly. Not loud. The kind of playful that makes you smile and lean in rather than roll your eyes. Micro-interactions that feel alive. Copy that has a wink in it. Restraint applied everywhere except where surprise creates delight.

**Emotional goal**: Visitors should leave thinking "this person actually gives a shit" — about their craft, about the user, about getting things right.

**Aspiration**: Position Fabricio as a Design Engineer (the "unicorn" role) — someone equally at home in Figma discussions and TypeScript PRs. The portfolio itself must be the proof.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Dark, warm, precise. Near-black backgrounds with a golden accent that feels warm rather than garish. Tight typography. Generous whitespace within sections. Dense where density serves, open where breathing room earns attention.

**References and what to take from each**:

- **Josh W. Comeau** (joshwcomeau.com): Personality in every word and interaction. Animations that teach and delight rather than distract. His site feels like a person, not a product. Take: the sense of genuine warmth in the details.
- **Maggie Appleton** (maggieappleton.com): Conceptual depth expressed through visual craft. Handcrafted details, thoughtful illustrations, prose that respects the reader's intelligence. Take: the idea that every element should have a _reason_, not just a role.
- **Emil Kowalski** (emilkowal.ski): Design Engineering as a discipline. Obsessive polish on interactions. Components that feel physically satisfying to use. Take: the bar for micro-interaction quality and the discipline of knowing when to stop.

**Anti-references**: Nothing that reads as generic dev portfolio (green terminal font, floating code snippets, "Hello World" hero). No over-designed agency site with parallax on every scroll. No corporate "results-driven professional" blandness. If it could belong to any other developer, it's wrong.

**Theme**: Dark-first. Light mode is a possible future enhancement but not a priority. The About section's white inversion is intentional contrast, not inconsistency.

**Color rules**: Golden accent (#ffc457 / hsl(39 100% 67%)) used sparingly and purposefully — it earns attention, so spend it wisely. Purple animation mid-stop creates depth. Avoid adding new accent colors without strong justification.

### Design Principles

1. **Earn attention fast, then reward it.** Visitors don't read — they scan and decide. Every section must justify its existence in the first glance. Once they're interested, the details (animations, copy, interactions) are what make them stay.

2. **Personality is a design choice.** The LEGO theme, the Fab coins, the direct copy — these are not decoration, they're the brand. Resist any impulse to "clean it up" into something safer or more generic. Safer is invisible.

3. **Craft lives in the things people notice without knowing why.** The spring on a button press. The underline animation on a nav link. The easing curve on a transition. These aren't nice-to-haves — they're the proof that Fabricio is a Design Engineer.

4. **Restraint over maximalism.** Every added animation, gradient, or visual element should justify itself. The references (Josh, Maggie, Emil) are not maximalist — they're precise. One great interaction beats five mediocre ones.

5. **Dark, warm, human.** The design should feel like you're in a workshop lit by warm light — focused, technical, but alive. Not a cold tech showcase. Not a sterile portfolio. A space that reflects a real person's aesthetic.
